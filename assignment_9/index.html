The Process of Podcast Production

Tips for a Better Tabletop Podcast Experience

Resources to Begin

  • Audio Recording
    • For actualplay-multiperson recording: ensure you have good quality mics for each individual.
    • Utilize a soundboard for to connect each microphone.
    • I recommend each cast member wear headphones that are connected to the audio output so they can hear themselves during the recording.
    • Note: If recording remotely each person should have a good quality mic, be in a quiet space and wear headphones to avoid echo.
  • Audio and Editing Software
    • Mix with: WavePad
    • Record with: Reaper
    • Edit with: Audacity
    • Each of these programs are easily accessable and free to use

#1. Comparing Quality Audio

Early Audio on Single Track Recording

Notice in the earlier bloopers version of The Emergence Podcast the audio quality echos and contains white noise. The players are often heard at difference volumes as well.

Recent Audio on Multi-Track Recording

Now listening to later versions of bloopers from The Emergence Podcast the quality is crisper and echo is reduced. We are able to isolate individual recording tracks and more

#2. Creating Thumbnails and Logos

Logos are an important tool to brand your content. Make it something recognizable and simplistic with multiple variations.

  • Making a Logo should be cohesive and have an appealing color scheme.
  • Notice each variation contains the same design or text, but is different enough that a brand can use each variation interchangably.

Thumbnails for our podcasts follow a few rules: They each should have a recognizable title and is designed in a similar style.

  • Our Thumbnails all have a signature lineless art style that is recognizable and consistant
  • Notice each variation feels like a DICEBERG Ahead! Thumbnail because the style and text can be grouped with other podcast titles.

#3. Producing a Finished Episode

Creating Finished content can be tedious, however there are a few simple reminders when recording and editing.

  • Its exteremely Important to enforce good mic etiquette when recording. You should be able to hear eachother clearly while also avoiding pops in speech or disruptive noise.
  • Always double check that you are recording correctly throughout each session and save in multiple places.
  • Don't forget to record extra production content like outros, promotions or introductions to your podcast.
  • When editing in Audacity keep your tracks organized
  • Once Exported, gather your Title, thumbnail and MP3 file and upload it to your chosen Podcast distribution platform Like Libsyn

#4. Marketing on Social Media

Posting content regularly to multiple social media platforms is the key way for people to discover your podcasts

  • Use websites like Kapwing or Reel to create snapshots of an episode or outtakes.
  • Even though podcasts are primarily an audio medium, visual aspects for socials is a must.
  • If your podcast doesn't record video as well, use your thumbnails or animate elements to make your video more engaging.
  • Aside from what you post, there should also be interactions and conversations with your audience or other creators to further your networking.

#5. Extra Content

There is no harm in posting more content if you are able. Here are some ideas for extra content:

  • Character or Promotional Art
  • Memes relating to your content niche
  • Outtakes/Bloopers
  • Interactive posts to engage your audience
  • GIVEAWAYS are excellent marketing tools to expand your listeners and feel like you are engaging your community.
  • Patreon is a great platform to gather support from your communtiy by providing extra content.

Check your local game stores in your area if they have TTRPG Events or are willing to promote your content
