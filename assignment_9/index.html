Tips for a Better Tabletop Podcast Experience

Resources to Begin

  • Audio Recording
    • For actualplay-multiperson recording: ensure you have good quality mics for each individual.
    • Utilize a soundboard for to connect each microphone.
    • I recommend each cast member wear headphones that are connected to the audio output so they can hear themselves during the recording.
    • Note: If recording remotely each person should have a good quality mic, be in a quiet space and wear headphones to avoid echo.
  • Audio and Editing Software
    • Mix with: WavePad
    • Record with: Reaper
    • Edit with: Audacity
    • Each of these programs are easily accessable and free to use

#2. Creating Thumbnails and Logos

Logos are an important tool to brand your content. Make it something recognizable and simplistic with multiple variations.

  • Making a Logo should be cohesive and have an appealing color scheme.
  • Notice each variation contains the same design or text, but is different enough that a brand can use each variation interchangably.

Thumbnails for our podcasts follow a few rules: They each should have a recognizable title and is designed in a similar style.

  • Our Thumbnails all have a signature lineless art style that is recognizable and consistant
  • Notice each variation feels like a DICEBERG Ahead! Thumbnail because the style and text can be grouped with other podcast titles.

Step 3: Gather your wood

make sure your wood is dry. If not, you can remove some of the damp bark with a pocket knife

  • place your tinder of choice in the center of your fire ring
  • gather pencil size sticks (and/or pinecones) and stack them in a cone shape around your tinder, leaving enough space for air
  • now gather larger sticks (about the size of your wrist) and form another cone
  • gather even larger sticks the size of your arm and larger and stack them in a safe area outside of your fire ring

Step 4: Lighting your fire

If your fire isn't starting, make sure you have followed all of the above steps

  • light your tinder on fire using your fire source
  • once all of your sticks in your fire ring have caught fire, you can slowly add larger sticks
  • slowly increase the size of sticks you add
  • once you reach sticks about leg size, you will need fewer of them and they will burn more slowly

Step 5: Extinguishing your fire

Make sure to fully extinguish the fire before leaving your campsite

  • Let the fire die out on it's own until it is just smoldering ash
  • carefully and slowly pour your bucket of water over the ashes - it will still be very hot!
  • keep pouring water until you no longer see smoke
  • bury the puddle with some dirt
  • stir the mixture with your shovel until you no longer see smoke
  • continue this process if it is still warm, or you still see smoke
